Child Protection Policy

Document Version: 1.0
Last Updated: November 2025

1. Introduction

Children represent the largest demographic group in India and are recognized as the nation's most valuable resource. The Constitution guarantees their rights and mandates protection from exploitation, abuse, neglect, and deprivation. DSP adopts a rights-based approach aligned with national and international frameworks ensuring every child grows in dignity and safety.

2. DSP Commitment

DSP recognizes every individual below 18 years as a child and acknowledges that childhood is critical for physical, emotional, psychological, and social development. The organization commits to safeguarding, supporting, and empowering children, especially those who are marginalized, vulnerable, or living in difficult circumstances.

3. Guiding Principles

The Child Protection Policy is built upon the following principles:

  • Every child has universal and indivisible human rights.
  • No child will face discrimination based on caste, religion, gender, disability, language, economic status, or origin.
  • Protection of the best interest of the child is the priority in all decisions.
  • Children have the right to participate and express their views regarding matters affecting them.
  • Children must grow in a safe family environment and shall not be separated unless necessary for their welfare.
  • Children must live free from violence, exploitation, abuse, and neglect.

4. Core Priority Areas

A. Survival, Health & Nutrition

DSP prioritizes optimal health and nutrition for all children and commits to ensuring:

  • Maternal and newborn care
  • Access to safe sanitation and clean drinking water
  • Prevention of disease and disability
  • Nutrition support for infants, children, adolescents, and mothers
  • Services and non-discriminatory access for children with HIV/AIDS or disabilities

B. Education & Development

Every child has the right to education, learning opportunities, and developmental support. DSP focuses on:

  • Inclusive early childhood education
  • Ensuring school enrollment and retention
  • Safe and child-friendly learning environments
  • Prevention of discrimination and corporal punishment
  • Access to vocational training and digital learning opportunities
  • Providing support for children outside school systems (child laborers, trafficked children, migrant children, orphans, etc.)

C. Protection

Children will be protected from all forms of harm, including:

  • Physical and sexual abuse
  • Trafficking and exploitation
  • Child labor
  • Neglect, abandonment, and violence
  • Cyber risks, pornography, and substance abuse

For children without parental care, DSP supports community and family-based solutions such as kinship care, adoption, or foster care — institutional care remains the last resort.

5. Advocacy and Partnerships

DSP will work collaboratively with:

  • Government institutions
  • Community groups
  • NGOs and civil society
  • Private sector and media

These partnerships promote awareness, respect for child rights, and strengthen protective systems across communities.

6. Implementation, Monitoring & Coordination

DSP ensures coordinated implementation of this policy through:

  • Active cooperation with government departments, especially the Ministry of Women & Child Development
  • Continuous monitoring through structured systems
  • Enforcement of rights in all programs, services, and outreach activities

7. Research, Documentation & Capacity Building

The organization promotes evidence-based practices through:

  • Child-focused data collection and research
  • Regular evaluations of child protection initiatives
  • Capacity building and mandatory sensitization of all personnel involved in child-related activities

8. Resource Allocation

DSP commits to allocating appropriate human, financial, and material resources to ensure effective implementation, transparency, and accountability. Child-focused budgeting will evaluate the impact of investment on children's well-being and protection.

9. Review Mechanism

The Child Protection Policy will be reviewed every five years in collaboration with stakeholders, including children, to ensure relevance, improvement, and compliance with emerging needs and legal standards.

Conclusion

This policy reinforces DSP's dedication to building a safe, just, nurturing, and empowering environment for every child. By prioritizing survival, development, education, protection, and participation, DSP ensures that children's rights are upheld and future generations are supported with dignity, equality, and opportunity.
